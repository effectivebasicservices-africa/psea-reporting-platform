We believe in dignity, safety, and respect for all. If you or someone you know has experienced or witnessed any form of sexual exploitation, abuse, or misconduct related to eBASE’s work, we are here to listen and act.

What Is Sexual Exploitation, Abuse, and Harassment?

eBASE maintains a zero-tolerance policy for any form of sexual misconduct. Below are the definitions and examples to help you understand what constitutes a violation under our PSEA (Prevention of Sexual Exploitation and Abuse) policy.

Sexual Exploitation

Defined as the act of taking advantage of an individual's vulnerability or power dynamics to engage in sexual activities for personal or third-party gain, without regard for the individual's rights, well-being, or consent.

Example: A team member hinting that a project participant will be selected for follow-up benefits or jobs if they "stay close" or "cooperate" in a personal way.

Sexual Abuse

Refers to any non-consensual sexual activity or behavior, including attempted or threatened acts.

  • A supervisor pressures a junior staff member for sex in exchange for job benefits.
  • A staff member makes sexual advances during work events.
  • Any sexual activity with minors (under 18) is strictly prohibited.

Sexual Harassment

Includes unwelcome or unwanted behavior of a sexual nature that causes discomfort, distress, or harm.

  • Sexual jokes
  • Repeated advances
  • Sharing explicit content
  • Unwanted touching or questions

None of these behaviors are ever acceptable.

What Happens After You Report?

  • Report Received: Logged securely by safeguarding focal point
  • Initial Review: Checked against PSEA criteria
  • Investigation: Confidential process begins if valid
  • Support: Survivors offered psychosocial, legal, and medical support
  • Outcome: Disciplinary action if confirmed
  • Feedback: Optional updates if contact was provided

No proof is required—only reasonable concern.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • Can I report anonymously? Yes.
  • Will the accused know I reported them? No. Identity is protected.
  • What if the accused is a senior staff or donor? Everyone is accountable.
  • Will I face retaliation? No. Retaliation is prohibited.
  • I witnessed something. Should I report? Yes. Bystander reports help prevent future abuse.

What If You're Accused?

  • You will be informed and given a chance to respond
  • An internal or external review will occur
  • Sanctions include suspension, termination, or referral
  • False, malicious accusations can lead to consequences

Survivor Support

  • Immediate safety planning
  • Health, psychosocial, and legal referrals
  • Emotional support and counselling
  • Survivor-led approach: Your needs come first
